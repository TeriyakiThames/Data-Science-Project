Abstract:

In this paper, a notched metal spiral spoof LSPs double-layer resonator structure is proposed, which is excited by microstrip lines and generates multipolar resonant modes, based on which a dielectric constant sensor is realized, and the microwave sensor operates in the frequency range of 3 GHz to 3.9 GHz, and the simulation results show that the sensor has the advantages of high sensitivity and good robustness, and has great potential in dielectric constant measurement. Contents

I. Introduction

Localized Surface Plasmons (LSPs) are localized electromagnetic wave modes confined to metal surfaces, arising from the photonic excitation of metal nanoparticles. These modes are sensitive to the size of the nanostructure and the surrounding environment, and they have a wide range of applications, including materials sensing.[1]-[3] The concept and application have been extended to spoof LSPs at microwave and terahertz frequencies using plasma metamaterials composed of subwavelength ripples, due to their enhanced sensitivity in the microwave band.[4]-[6] In this paper, we propose a bilayer structure of a metal-scored, grooved, helical spoof LSP resonator with microstrip line excitation in the lower structure that can generate multipolar resonant modes. Based on this design, we propose a sensor for dielectric constant measurement. Using the HFSS simulation model, we analyze the electric field distribution in the multipolar mode, select the appropriate electric field mode, and verify the sensors performance. The results demonstrate a significant change in resonance frequency when the dielectric constant of the material under test (MUT) changes.
